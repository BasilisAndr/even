\ref Recorded on 22 June 2007 in Esso by Brigitte Pakendorf. The recording was done in the little ethnographic museum, with Aleksej Ichanga and his
friend Albert Adukanov going through the exhibit, Aleksej mainly explaining things, but Albert pitching in as well. In 2007, only about 4 minutes
were transcribed, translated and glossed, and then in 2016 BP undertook to finish the entire recording. Unfortunately, in 2007 the video. although
digitized in .avi format, was not converted to an .mpeg format, and in 2016 BP didn't have the video file with her (since she hadn't thought she would
be working on this when she left for the field). This made the transcription and translation a bit difficult at times, since it's not always clear what they're
talking about. The file for the first four minutes is "Ichanga_Adukanov_museum" (only Toolbox), which is followed by
"Ichanga_Adukanov_muzej1_cont" (ELAN and Toolbox); this is the final bit of recording.
\qu In 2016, transcribed by BP, transcription checked and Russian translation by Rimma Maksimovna Egorova, glossing by BP. Funding by the Max Planck
Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics) and CNRS/Université Lyon 2 (laboratoire DDL). Speaker abbreviations = AEI, ASA
\ref Aleksej Evstropovich Ichanga was 46 at the time of recording. He was working in the ethnographic museum at the time. He had always been
hard of hearing, which explains why he often doesn't hear what Albert says and therefore asks ("hunh?"), and it also explains his intonation. He is
the brother of Tamara Evstropovna Bataxaeva.
\ref  Al'bert SergeeviÄ Adukanov was 45 at the time of recording. He has since passed away.
