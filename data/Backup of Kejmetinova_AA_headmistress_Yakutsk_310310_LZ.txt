know the word erimetče, so it wasn't possible to check whether the glossing is correct
\gr she later on remembered that her mother had used a verb erimet- with a meaning of 'boletj', and there is also a form eripkečenteken in
Emcheni (88), and at first we thought that those two verbs might be connected, erimetče 'being ill' and the eripkeč- form possibly meaning
'hurt', since the baby cried when the bird scratched him; so I thought maybe there is a root eri- 'boletj, be.ill/hurt' from which these two verbs
might be derived; however, on 080312, when I discussed this with her (various_quest_1, very beginning) she did point out that the form
eripkečenteken means the baby is doing something while crying, and if it had been that he started to cry from pain (zaplakal ot boli), then it
should have been the anterior converb that was used; plus, it's not really clear what the suffixes would be that are added to eri- to derive
eripkeč-, so IVK suggested that it probably is rather two different verbs that aren't connected
