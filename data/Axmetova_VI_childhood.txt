\ref Recorded on 7 July 2007 in Anavgai by Brigitte Pakendorf in the speaker's kitchen. Only BP was present, which probably explains the many
code switches to Russian. The recording came after several days of working together, during which VIA had told BP some things about
herself, so that there was already some familiarity between the two, plus VIA was used to having the microphone pointed at her.
\qu Transcribed by BP, transcription checked and Russian translation by Valentina Innokent'evna Axmetova, glossing by BP. Funding by the Max Planck
Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics). Speaker abbreviation = VIA
\ref Valentina Innokent'evna Axmetova was 67 years old at the time of recording. She had 8 years of schooling, then worked in the kindergarten,
in the collective farm, and as a tent worker (chumrabotnitsa) and herder in the reindeer herd. Her first two husbands were Russians, and she
speaks Russian with her daughters; her third husband was an Even and they spoke Even together. VIA had a serious stroke in 2016 that has
left her incapable of speech.
