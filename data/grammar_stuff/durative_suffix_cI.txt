\id elicitation_080312_quest2_01
\ref
\gr I had noticed for a long time that there were pairs of verbs with the same/similar meaning, where one had a suffix -čI, and
I do think after eliciting it with IVK that one should postulate a new "durative" suffix
\nt
\gr of course, at first glance one might think that this -čI is just a variant of the resultative -Č; however, the resultative form
of mịan- "be surprised" (which doesn't exist) should be mịanat-, and not mịaŋčị-
\nt
\gr however, it should probably be a suffix not -čI, but rather -(k)čI, because there is neː- and neːkči-, and several of the verbs
ending in -n (like mịan-) are then -ŋčI, e.g. mịaŋčị-
\nt
\gr another argument in favour of a new suffix is that you cannot have two resultatives following on each other, but there is
the form ọːčịt- 'repair, renovate' derived from ọː- 'make' -, which has a resultative -t, so that the -čị should be something
else (in this form, obviously, there's also an element of lexicalization, because ọːčị- doesn't exactly mean "make for a long
time"
