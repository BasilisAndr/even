\id 000_intro_from_Udihe
\tx
\nt Nikolaeva & Tolskaya (p.135) identify 5 functions of Udihe “alienable” poss suffix: 1) temporary ownership:  this is the function that is always illustrated, “my
boar’s head, your fish” 2) alienable possession: a few alienable nouns obligatorily take this suffix – ‘cow’, ‘money’, ‘land’, ‘tree with meaning firewood’ 3) abstract
associative relationship through certain activity: situational reference, things like ‘my deer (which I shot, followed, raised)’, ‘my father’s deer’ – the one he shot, or
was simply talking about’, Japanese candle – candle given by a Japanese; often attaches to geographical terms and denotes place of living; but also particular
relation through shared activity, or person well-known to both hearer and speaker (with personal names) 4) substitutional meaning ‘instead of something’: denotes
non-typical object used for a funtion (give me ‘my’ pillow = give me something as a pillow) 5) predicative possession: but here suffix attaches to possessor and not
possessum; I used to think it’s homonymy, but now I'm not so sure
