\ref
\gr Although the grammars seem to distinguish between -DʒIŋe 'hypothetical participle' and -DIŋe 'future participle', here in
Sebjan these seem to be allomorphs; after consonants for the Future Imperative 1SG only the variant -DʒIŋe is used,
while after vowels -D(I)ŋE is mostly used, though -DʒIŋe is also sometimes used.

\gr I'm not at all sure about the "herŋe" - at first I had analyzed it as the "negative modal converb", but that supposedly
only occurs in periphrastic expressions with negative verbs. After further elicitation (cf. below) I figured it might be the
polite imperative suffix, because the above sentence is synonymous with forms with the hypothetical participle.
\nt Checked this form (elicited with Ija Vasil'evna Krivoshapkina, sebjan070308_3:35): it cannot be used without
"goːniken", and it is synonymous with the hypothetical participle. However, when I asked her a day later
(sebjan080308_25:10), she at first said that it was the negative modal converb (i.e the converb that occurs with verbs
like turkut), but then also pointed out that it is synonymous with the hypothetical participle, and then agreed with me
that it might also be the polite imperative, perhaps both.

\mb ịak -W   ga   -ŋE     goːn -nIkEn   dʒọm  -kEČ -R(E)   -m   = ịak -W   ga   -DʒIŋE -J
