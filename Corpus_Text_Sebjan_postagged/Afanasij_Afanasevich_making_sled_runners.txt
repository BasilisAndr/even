\ref Recorded on 18 March 2009 in the little winter hut of the 9th reindeer brigade near Sebjan-Küöl (about 1-1.5 hour's drive) by Brigitte
Pakendorf. Afanasij Afanasevič commented on a photo showing the preparation of a sled runner; unfortunately, the hut filled up as we
were doing this, so there is quite a bit of background noise, a baby crying and gurgling, people talking quite loudly, a truck passing by,
fire crackling

Max Planck Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics). Speaker abbreviation = AAS
\ref Afanasij Afanas'evič Stepanov was 57 years old at the time of recording, used to be a reindeer herder, then glava of the village, owned one
of the shops in the village at the time of recording. Brother of Zoja Afanas'evna Stepanova.

\gr tọg is 'fire' as in 'flames', something burning, and the Even word gụlụn means 'campfire', the fire inside the house; the Sakha copy ọtụː has
the same meaning as gụlụn, it is the 'campfire', as can be seen from the Russian translation; in theory it is possible to say 'gụlụn tọgan' the
campfire's fire; (file 09_quest_2010, 10:10) tọg is the word for 'flame', e.g. hibeči tọgan 'candle flame', and they don't say nuri or tọg enŋen
here; for nergi Ija thought it might be soot or sooty smoke (these latter 3 words are from Cincius)
