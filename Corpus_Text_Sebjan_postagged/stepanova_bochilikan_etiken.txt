\ref Recorded on 01 March 2009 in Sebjan-Küöl by Brigitte Pakendorf, 
at Zoja Afanas'evna's kitchen table; had sent away her daughter and
grandson so that it was very nice and quiet. Only BP was present.
She had written down the fairytale beforehand (in order to get it all
right) and more or less read it off; was a conscious effort to speak in
the local dialect (but has numerous standard Even elements)
\qu Transcribed by BP in Elan with the help of speaker's notes; a very free
translation into Russian given by Zoja Afanas'evna herself, later on a more
accurate sentence-by-sentence translation into Russian was given by Ija
Vasil'evna Krivoshapkina. Glossed by BP. Speaker abbreviation = ZAS
\ref Zoja Afanas'evna Stepanova was 59 years old at time of recording,
primary school teacher, social pedagogue (socpedagog) and leader of
folklore ensemble Merlenke, very much in favour of
literary/standard Even language, feels that there are not many
differences between different dialects, mutual intelligibility, very
much against all the mixing of Even and Sakha here in Sebjan (“eto
ne Lamunxinskij govor”), very interested in Even folklore and
language. Sister of Afanasij Afanas'evič Stepanov
