\ref Recorded on 23 March 2010 in Sebjan-Küöl by Brigitte Pakendorf and Natalia Aralova. This session was initially set up by LAT's sister, NAS, who
had told about her work in the nomadic school 2 weeks previously. NAS had recommended her older sister as a narrator of the family history, and
had contacted her sister. LAT came to the flat where NA and BP are staying by prior arrangement, in the company of her daughter, a temporary
teacher at the Sebjan school who understands but does not speak Even.  LAT was very diffident and unsure about what to say in Even and
frightened of the camera. After an initial period in which we just discussed the project and in which she told various little bits of family history in
Russian, we set up the recording equipment in the kitchen, with the daughter sitting opposite LAT.  While she was clearly very nervous in the
beginning, and twice interrupted herself to comment on her use of a Sakha discourse particle, after a while she was calmer and narrated for nearly
half an hour. It was very quiet during the recording, with the exception of a dog barking and a truck going by; also, NA got up once to check the
state of the video camera, so that floorboards creak.
\qu Transcribed by BP in 2012, transcription checked and Russian translation by Ija Vasil'evna Krivoshapkina, glossing by BP. Funding by the Volkswagen
Foundation (DoBeS grant). Speaker abbreviation = LAT.
\ref Lidija Alekseevna Tulasynova was born in March 1953, so she was 57 at the time of recording. She has no higher education beyond 10 years of
schooling, and worked variously in the kindergarten, the post office, and as a tent worker (chumrabotnitsa) in the reindeer herd. She is the sister of
Nadezhda Alekseevna Stepanova.
