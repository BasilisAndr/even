\ref Recorded on 14 March 2009, in the evening, in the winter house of the 10th reindeer brigade in a place called Bajindja, by Brigitte
Pakendorf. The siblings Sergej Egorovich Nikitin (32 years old), Evgenij Egorovich Nikitin (36 yrs) and Anna Egorovna Nikitina
(39 yrs) were sitting around the table with BP as well as their 12-year-old nephew Mitja. Different people spoke at different times,
and the sound file was cut into different narratives. One can hear the fire crackling, and the voices of other men in the
background, drinking and talking – they got quite loud at times, and Anna and Evgenij and Mitja sometimes got up to shush them
\qu Transcription with pen and paper by Ija Vasilevna Krivoshapkina, transcription in ELAN by BP, Russian translation by IVK, glossing by BP.
Funding by Max Planck Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics). Speaker abbreviation = SEN
\ref Anna has worked as a chumrabotnitsa most of her life, right after school, so she only has her school education; Evgenij started to
study twice in his younger years but broke off to return to reindeer herding, currently he is studying zaochno at the Selxos
Akademija to become a zootexnik; Sergej has a diplom in music (sort of kultrabotnik, I think) and is currently studying zaochno,
doing something like Volkskunde, the culture of the local peoples, mainly Yakut culture. The siblings are the children of Anastasija
Xristoforovna Krivoshapkina.
