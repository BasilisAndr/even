\ref Recorded on 24 March 2010 by Brigitte Pakendorf in Sebjan-Küöl, in the afternoon, in the living room of Krivoshapkina's house. This was the
second attempt to record AXK in 2010. I had first gone a week earlier, when we had chatted and had tea; unfortunately, after about 2 hours of
socializing she had said that her head had started to hurt badly, and I therefore left without any recording. On this second attempt we recorded
first and chatted later. I recorded three separate narratives, the first one of ethnographic interest, the latter two about the history of the village.
The recording was done in the living room of AXK's house, and I was the only adult present. During this recording (the second one) a
granddaughter can be heard walking around and at one point she peered into the camera. Also, AXK's sick husband can occasionally be heard
coughing in a back room, and there might be some dogs barking in the background; furthermore, the phone rang once or twice.
\qu Transcribed by BP in 2012, translated by Ija Vasil'evna Krivoshapkina, glossed by BP. Funding by the Volkswagen Foundation (DoBeS grant). Speaker
abbreviation = AXK
\ref Anastasija Xristoforovna Krivoshapkina is the mother of the Nikitiny (Anna Egorovna and Sergej Egorovič) and the grandmother of Mitja
Nikitin. She was 65 years old at the time of recording, had worked as a nurse, then as a chumrabotnitsa.
