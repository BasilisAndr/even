\ref Recorded on 22 June 2007 in Esso by Brigitte Pakendorf. The recording was done in the little ethnographic museum, with Aleksej Ichanga and his friend
Albert Adukanov going through the exhibit, Aleksej mainly explaining things, but Albert pitching in as well. In 2007, only about 4 minutes were
transcribed, translated and glossed in Toolbox (i.e. no time-aligned ELAN file exists for this). Unfortunately, in 2007 the video. although digitized in .avi
format, was not converted to an .mpeg format, and I don't think we looked at the video while transcribing and translating, This made the transcription
and translation a bit difficult at times, since it's not always clear what they're talking about. The rest of the recording was transcribed, translated and
glossed in 2016; these are the files "Ichanga_Adukanov_muzej1_cont" and "Ichanga_Adukanov_muzej2".
\qu Transcribed by Aleksej Ichanga, translated by Valentina Innoken'evna Axmetova, glossed by BP. Transcription checked in 2016 with Rimma Maksimovna Egorova,
who understood AEI better than VIA did. Funding by the Max Planck Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics). Speaker abbreviations = AEI, ASA
\ref Aleksej Evstropovich Ichanga was 46 at the time of recording. He was working in the ethnographic museum at the time. He had always been
hard of hearing, which explains why he often doesn't hear what Albert says and therefore asks ("hunh?"), and it also explains his intonation. He is
the brother of Tamara Evstropovna Bataxaeva and Julia Evstropovna Tylkanova.
\ref  Al'bert Sergeevič Adukanov was 45 at the time of recording. He has since passed away.
