\ref Recorded on 10 June 2007 in Esso by Brigitte Pakendorf in the house of the speakers. Only the speakers and BP were
present. They spoke on different topics (prompted by questions by BP), mainly EIA speaking, but then EPA took over
with this narrative, during which EIA left to buy cigarettes.
\qu Transcribed (pen and paper) by Aleksej Evstropovich Ichanga, transcription checked and Russian translation by Valentina
Innokent'evna Axmetova, glossing by BP. Funding by the Max Planck Society (MPRG on Comparative Population Linguistics).
Speaker abbreviations = EIA, EPA
\ref Efim Innokent'evich Amganov was born in 1952, i.e. he was 54-55 at the time of the recording. He had a college-level
education and worked as a driver before going on pension. He passed away in 2012.
\ref Elena Petrovna Amganova was born in 1952, i.e. she was 54-55 at the time of the recording. She had 8 years of schooling
and had worked as a milker and then a cleaner.
