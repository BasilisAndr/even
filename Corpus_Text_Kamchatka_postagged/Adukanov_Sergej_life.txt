\ref Recorded on 13 July 2009 in Esso by Brigitte Pakendorf. The recording was done on Monday morning in the men's changing room in the
'ballet room' of the 'House of Culture' in Esso, which is on the top floor. It was fairly quiet, but the accoustics (high ceiling, stone walls, empty
room) left to be desired. Only SPA and BP were present; in the beginning SPA nervously rubbed his feet together, which made a squeaky
sound. 
\qu Transcription and Russian translation in Word by Rimma Maksimovna Egorova, time-aligned in ELAN by Luise Zippel. Glossed by BP. Funding by the
Volkswagen Foundation (DoBeS grant). Speaker abbreviation = SPA.
\ref Sergej Petrovich Adukanov was born in 1958, i.e. he was 51 at the time of recording. He grew up in northern Kamchatka, so that he is fluent
in both Koryak and Even; he then studied in Yakutsk, where he acquired a knowledge of Yakut. He worked first as a vet in the reindeer
herds, then as a scientific assistant in the ethnographic museum in Esso, and is now working as the choir master of the folklore ensemble
Nulgur (where he also sings and dances).
