\ref Recorded on 12 July 2009 by Natalia Aralova and Brigitte Pakendorf. Since during the first recording session
(Grigoreva_Natalia_Ionovna_shkola_remesel) NIG got somewhat carried away, telling us about her life and giving a Christian sermon,
she hadn't managed to tell the things she had actually prepared - fairy tales and a legend and a bit about the local toponymy. We
therefore arranged for a second recording session, which took place 2 weeks after the first one in the flat that BP and NA are renting
in Esso. We recorded in the living room, and had switched off the fridge and all telephones, so it was fairly quiet with the exception of
the occasional car going by outside the window, or a group of children shouting. This session was even longer than the first, taking 4
hours in total; however, there was a break in between to drink tea, and later NIG also went to the shops to buy some cigarettes, the
actual recording time was 2 hours. NIG had prepared herself beforehand, and had brought written versions (Russian and Even) of
the stories and fairy tales she wanted to tell, which she used mainly as a prompt. BP monitored the Marantz while NA monitored the
video camera.
\qu In 2016, transcribed by BP, transcription checked and Russian translation by Rimma Maksimovna Egorova, glossing by BP. Funding by the
Volkswagen Foundation (DoBeS grant) and CNRS/Universit� Lyon 2 (laboratoire DDL). Speaker abbreviation = NIG
\ref Natalja Ionovna Grigor'eva (Ichanga) was born in 1956, i.e. she was 53-54 at the time of the recording. She had first trained as a
leather worker, but later gone on to get a higher education as an actor and director of spectacles. She speaks Russian with her
husband and children.
